Kerala History: Political Parties

The political organisation of parties in India draws their power from the constitution of India. Kerala history: political parties are an essential topic of knowledge. It is important to learn about regional and localized histories as they are rich with diversity and multifaceted knowledge. Kerala history: political parties are the main subject of concern in the article. In Kerala, there are two types of political parties. These two parties along with their associated aspects form the subject of Kerala history: political parties. The two big coalitions of parties are the UDF or the United Democratic Front and the LDF or the Left Democratic Front. The coalition is an integral part of Kerala’s history. The coalition is a strategy of political party politics. Coalition refers to a situation when a government is formed based on several parties merging. This leads to the formation of a bigger party. This situation happens when no singular party has attained majority votes in an election. A coalition shares similar political values, objectives, or ideologies. 

Political Party in India

The political party in India draws its powers and rights from the constitution. The constitution of India lays down a democratic basis for the semi-federal and semi-unitary political formations. The political parties in India and more widely, in any country, are the representatives of the people of a constituency or a state. There are state-level and national-level political parties in India. The formation of the political party in India largely owes to the early politics of the Congress. The political party in India is numbered around 2585. There are 7 national-level parties. 54 of these parties are recognised and belong to specific states. In Kerala, there are two big types of political parties.      The two big coalitions of parties are the UDF or the United Democratic Front and the LDF or the Left Democratic Front. These political parties are in existence since the 1960s as well as the 1970s. The political party in India. A political party in India is only recognised if it is approved by the ECI or the Election Commission of India. To know which political party has recognition, ECI hosts the information. 

National Parties in India

National parties in India are those political parties that are recognised by the ECI and have a large presence in more than two states. So, the parties in Kerala are nationally recognised parties since these parties also have a political presence in more than two states. National parties are bigger and have many members. There are in total 7 national parties in India. National political parties in India have specific symbols and can compete in the general elections. There are three specific conditions that a party has to meet if it wishes to be amongst the national parties in India:

  • Has state party recognition in more than 4 states
  • Has secured 2% in Lok Sabha seats from more than 3 states
  • Has secured 6% and above in the polls from more than 4 states

  • CPI (M)

The smaller parties within a coalition share their resources. Kerala’s political system in Kerala history: political parties, is interesting due to their political features. The legislative power of Kerala lies with the central Legislative Assembly. It is a unicameral body. Such a system is provided under the federal system of governance of the Indian constitution. Politics in Kerala is well-known for its left-leaning. Marxist and centrist Marxist ideologies are very popular in Keralan political system. Kerala is also known for having the only communist elected government in Asia.
